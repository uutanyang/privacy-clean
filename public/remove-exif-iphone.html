// FAQ
What EXIF data does an iPhone store?
iPhone photos contain GPS coordinates, camera model (e.g. iPhone 15 Pro Max), lens info, aperture, ISO, focal length, flash status, date/time with timezone, software version (iOS 18.x), and face detection regions. All of this can reveal your location and device information when you share photos.
Can someone find my location from an iPhone photo?
Yes. If Location Services is enabled for the Camera app, every photo embeds exact GPS coordinates. Anyone who receives your original photo file can extract this data and find exactly where the photo was taken — down to the building.
Does removing EXIF affect photo quality?
No. PrivacyClean uses WASM-powered lossless stripping — we remove only the metadata segments, not the image data. Your photos remain pixel-for-pixel identical. No re-encoding, no compression, no quality loss whatsoever.
Does iOS strip EXIF when sharing photos?
iOS offers to remove location data when sharing via Messages, Mail, or AirDrop. However, this only removes GPS — other EXIF data like camera model, timestamps, and lens info remain. PrivacyClean removes ALL metadata, not just GPS.
How is this different from screenshotting a photo?
Screenshotting removes metadata but also reduces quality and resolution. PrivacyClean strips metadata without any quality loss — your photos stay at full resolution. It's the best of both worlds.
